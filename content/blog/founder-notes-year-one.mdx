Founding UIU Data Science Club was, in retrospect, mostly a series of small decisions that compounded.

## What worked

- A project gallery instead of a CV-style member list
- Weekly critique sessions — failure modes became the curriculum
- Partnering with industry for real mentors, not just logos

## What I'd change

- Start with a smaller, sharper scope. We did too much in year one.
- Make the onboarding harder. Lowering the bar lowered the average output.
- Hire a co-organizer earlier. Founding fatigue is real.

The thing nobody tells you: the community you build is the work. Not the events, not the slides, not the partnerships. The community is what survives.
