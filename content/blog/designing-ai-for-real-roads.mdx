A demo on a curated dataset is one thing. A detector that runs on a busy Bangladeshi junction at noon is another. Here are the things I wish someone had told me earlier.

## The data shape is the bottleneck

If your training set doesn't look like your deployment footage, your mAP doesn't matter. Public benchmarks tend to be clean, well-lit, and shot from a fixed camera. Local roads are none of those.

We ended up building our own small, messy, annotated set. It paid off more than any architecture change.

## Thresholds beat architectures

Most "detector not working" complaints are threshold complaints. Per-class confidence calibration gets you 80% of the production-quality improvement you actually need.

## Latency is a feature

A correct detector at 4 FPS is useless for live enforcement. A slightly less accurate detector at 24 FPS is fine. Optimize for what the human in the loop can actually use.
